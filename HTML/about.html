The whole picture About Me

I was born and raised in the Phoenix Valley in the state of Arizona. Growing up I loved going to school, practicing the piano and playing outside. In highschool I enjoyed learning about a variety of different subjects, my favorites being humanities, and economics. Outside of class I discovered a passion for music, especially for writing songs and performing them live. I started a band with some friends to play the songs I was writing and we ended up playing a few shows and releasing an album of our original songs. I also loved playing volleyball as an outside hitter on the highschool team.

After graduating highschool and a year of introductory business classes at Brigham Young University, I decided to serve a two year proselyting mission for my church. I was assigned to teach in Saltillo, Mexico and learned spanish. After 9 months of living in Mexico and learning spanish, making friends, teaching about Jesus, and loving the culture my time in Mexico was cut short by the COVID 19 pandemic. I spent some time with my family then went back out to Atlanta, Georgia to finish my two years as a missionary. In my time in Mexico and Georgia I learned valuable skills of putting other peoples' needs before my own, how to have crucial conversations, and working long hours to acheive goals.


Now I am back at BYU studying Finance and Social Impact. I have learned alot about basic finance principles from my classes. In my Social Impact Project class I was able to work with Village Capitaland help them significantly improve their investor coalitions. From that experience I learned how to tackle complex business issues with creative problem solving and analysis.


Now I'm looking forward to more opportunities to apply the skills I'm learning and solve real world business problems!
